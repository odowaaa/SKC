## 💬 Conversation

**At a Restaurant**

> **Waiter:** Good afternoon! What would you like to eat?
> **Customer:** I would like rice and chicken, please.
> **Waiter:** Would you like something to drink?
> **Customer:** Yes, I would like a cup of tea.
> **Waiter:** Anything else?
> **Customer:** No, thank you. That is all.
> **Waiter:** Okay. Your food will come soon.
> **Customer:** Thank you very much.

[Illustration: A friendly waiter taking an order from a customer at a small Somali restaurant table, notepad in hand, warm and inviting restaurant interior.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation. Order your own favorite food and drink.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: "I want" and "I would like"

We use **"I want"** and **"I would like"** to ask for something. "I would like" is more polite.

| Less Polite | More Polite |
|---|---|
| I want rice. | I would like rice, please. |
| I want water. | I would like some water, please. |

**✅ Practice:** Rewrite each sentence to make it more polite.

1. I want tea. → __________
2. I want chicken. → __________
3. I want juice. → __________

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** The "ch" sound in "chicken" and the short "i" in "fish," "milk."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat:
"I would like some rice, please."
"I am hungry. I am thirsty."
"This food is delicious!"

**Tip:** Practice linking "would like" smoothly: "I'd like" is a common short form used in speech.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "For breakfast, I eat anjero and drink tea. For lunch, I eat rice with chicken. For dinner, I eat pasta with soup. I am always hungry before dinner!"

**Listening Questions:**
1. What does the speaker eat for breakfast?
2. What does the speaker eat for lunch?
3. What does the speaker eat for dinner?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> My family eats three meals a day. In the morning, we eat anjero with honey and drink hot tea. At lunch, we eat rice with meat and vegetables. In the evening, we eat a light dinner, like soup or pasta. My favorite meal is lunch because I like rice very much.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What do they eat in the morning?
2. What do they eat at lunch?
3. What is the writer's favorite meal? Why?

[Illustration: A three-panel comic showing the same family at breakfast, lunch, and dinner tables throughout the day, matching each meal described in the reading passage.]

## 🎲 Games

**Game 1 — Food Basket:** Students sit in a circle. One says, "In my basket, I have rice," the next repeats and adds another food: "In my basket, I have rice and bananas," and so on.

**Game 2 — Menu Matching Game:** Match price cards to food picture cards to "order" a full meal within a budget.
