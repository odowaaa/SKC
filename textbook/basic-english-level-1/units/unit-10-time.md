# UNIT 10: TIME

[Illustration: A busy morning scene in a Somali household — a wall clock showing 6:30, a mother preparing breakfast, children getting ready for school, all synchronized around the clock. Flat vector illustration, warm morning light, teal and gold accents.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Tell time using o'clock, half past, and quarter
- Ask "What time is it?"
- Talk about your daily schedule
- Use time-of-day vocabulary correctly
- Read a simple timetable

## 💬 Conversation

**Asking for the Time**

> **Amina:** Excuse me, what time is it?
> **Faisal:** It is half past nine.
> **Amina:** Thank you! What time does class start?
> **Faisal:** Class starts at ten o'clock.
> **Amina:** Oh no, I am early. That's good!
> **Faisal:** Yes, it is better to be early than late.

[Illustration: Two students standing near a large wall clock showing 9:30, one checking a wristwatch, both looking relieved and cheerful in a school hallway.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation with different times.

## 📐 Grammar Point: Telling Time

We use **"It is"** to tell time:

| Time | Sentence |
|---|---|
| 7:00 | It is seven o'clock. |
| 7:15 | It is quarter past seven. |
| 7:30 | It is half past seven. |
| 7:45 | It is quarter to eight. |

We ask: **"What time is it?"** or **"What time does ___ start?"**

**✅ Practice:** Write the time in words.

1. 8:00 → __________
2. 8:30 → __________
3. 8:15 → __________
4. 8:45 → __________

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Linking "o'clock" smoothly: "seven-o'clock" sounds like one word.

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says a time; students repeat quickly and clearly.
"It is seven o'clock." "It is half past nine." "It is quarter to five."

**Tip:** Practice saying "quarter" clearly — many learners drop the "r" sound. Say: "kwar-ter."

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "I wake up at six o'clock. I eat breakfast at half past six. My class starts at eight o'clock. I eat lunch at quarter past twelve. I go home at four o'clock."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What time does the speaker wake up?
2. What time does class start?
3. What time is lunch?
4. What time do they go home?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> My name is Ahmed. Every day, I wake up at six o'clock in the morning. I pray, and then I eat breakfast at half past six. I go to SKC at quarter to eight. My English class starts at eight o'clock. I finish class at eleven o'clock. I am never late for class.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What time does Ahmed wake up?
2. What time does he eat breakfast?
3. What time does his English class start?
4. What time does he finish class?

## ✅ Practice

**A. Matching:** Match the clock time to its written form.

| Clock Shows | Written Time |
|---|---|
| 1. 4:00 | a. quarter past four |
| 2. 4:15 | b. four o'clock |
| 3. 4:30 | c. half past four |

**B. Circle the Correct Word:** It is (quarter / half) past six. It is (early / late) — the bus already left.

**C. Unscramble:** 1. it / o'clock / seven / is → __________ 2. time / what / is / it → __________
