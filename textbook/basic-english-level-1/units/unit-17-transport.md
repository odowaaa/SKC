# UNIT 17: TRANSPORT

[Illustration: A busy Somali street scene with many kinds of transport — a colorful bus, a yellow taxi, a bicycle, a motorcycle, and a small truck, all moving along a road with pedestrians crossing safely. Flat vector illustration, dynamic and colorful.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name types of transport
- Ask "How do you go to school/work?"
- Use "by" with transport ("by bus," "by car")
- Buy a ticket and ask about fares
- Follow simple traffic instructions

## 💬 Conversation

**Taking the Bus**

> **Passenger:** Excuse me, does this bus go to the market?
> **Driver:** Yes, it does. Please get on.
> **Passenger:** How much is the fare?
> **Driver:** The fare is 20 shillings.
> **Passenger:** Here you go. Thank you.
> **Driver:** You're welcome. The bus will stop near the market.

[Illustration: A passenger boarding a colorful public bus, handing coins to the driver, other passengers seated inside, market visible through the window.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation using different destinations and fares.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: "By" + Transport

We use **"by"** to describe how we travel:

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| by + transport | I go to school by bus. |
| | She travels by car. |
| | We go by bicycle. |

**Exception:** We say "on foot" or "I walk," not "by walk."

**✅ Practice:** Complete the sentences.

1. I go to school __________ bus.
2. My father goes to work __________ car.
3. I go to the market __________ foot. (on/by)

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** The "b" sound in "bus," "bicycle," "boat."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat:
"I go to school by bus." "She rides a bicycle." "The taxi is fast."

**Tip:** Practice saying "by bus" and "by boat" clearly — the "b" sound should be crisp and clear.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "Every day, I walk to the bus station. Then I take the bus to school. The fare is 15 shillings. After school, I walk home again."

**Listening Questions:**
1. How does the speaker get to the bus station?
2. What transport do they take to school?
3. How much is the fare?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> I live far from SKC, so I take the bus every day. The bus station is near my house. I walk there in five minutes. The bus ride takes thirty minutes, and the fare is 25 shillings. Sometimes, when the bus is late, I take a taxi instead. I always arrive at school on time.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. How does the writer usually travel to SKC?
2. How long does it take to walk to the bus station?
3. How much is the bus fare?
4. What does the writer do if the bus is late?

[Illustration: A student walking to a bus station near their home, then boarding a bus, with a small inset showing a taxi as a backup option, matching the reading passage's sequence.]

## 🎲 Games

**Game 1 — Transport Sorting:** Sort picture cards into categories: "Travels on Road," "Travels on Water," "Travels in Air."

**Game 2 — Traffic Light Game:** Teacher calls "Red!" (stop), "Yellow!" (walk slowly), "Green!" (go) — students act out the correct movement.
