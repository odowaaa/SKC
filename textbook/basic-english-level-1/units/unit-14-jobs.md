# UNIT 14: JOBS

[Illustration: A colorful collage of adult Somali workers in different professions — a teacher at a whiteboard, a nurse with a stethoscope, a driver next to a bus, a farmer with crops, and a shopkeeper at a stall — all shown working proudly. ### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name common jobs and occupations
- Ask and answer "What is your job?"
- Use "He/She works as..."
- Talk about where people work
- Describe your own job or future career

## 💬 Conversation

**Talking About Jobs**

> **Amina:** What is your job?
> **Faisal:** I am a driver. I drive a bus every day.
> **Amina:** That's interesting! What about your mother?
> **Faisal:** My mother is a nurse. She works at the hospital.
> **Amina:** What job do you want in the future?
> **Faisal:** I want to be an engineer. What about you?
> **Amina:** I want to be a teacher.

[Illustration: Two students chatting during a break, one describing his job as a driver with a small bus icon nearby, both engaged and smiling.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation. Talk about your own job or dream job.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: "Work as" / "A / An" + Job

We describe jobs using **"is a/an"** or **"works as a/an"**:

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| is + a/an + job | She is a nurse. |
| works as + a/an + job | He works as a driver. |

Remember: use **"an"** before jobs starting with a vowel sound (an engineer, an electrician).

**✅ Practice:** Complete the sentences.

1. My father is __________ (a/an) farmer.
2. My sister works __________ (as a/as an) teacher.
3. He is __________ (a/an) engineer.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Word stress in job names: "TEACHer," "docTOR," "enginEER."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat, clapping on the stressed syllable.
"TEACHer." "DRIver." "enginEER." "police OFficer."

**Tip:** Notice that some job words have stress at the beginning, and some at the end.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "My father is a farmer. He grows crops every day. My mother is a nurse. She works at the hospital. My brother is a driver. He drives a taxi in the city."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What is the father's job?
2. What is the mother's job?
3. What is the brother's job?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> My name is Hassan. I am a mechanic. I fix cars every day at a small garage. My work is hard, but I like it. My wife is a teacher. She teaches young children at a primary school. Together, we work hard for our family.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What is Hassan's job?
2. Where does he work?
3. What is his wife's job?
4. Who does she teach?

[Illustration: Hassan working under a car in a small garage, with tools around him, and a small inset picture of his wife teaching children in a classroom, matching the reading passage.]
