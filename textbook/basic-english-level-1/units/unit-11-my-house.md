# UNIT 11: MY HOUSE

[Illustration: A cutaway view of a modest, welcoming Somali house showing multiple rooms at once — a bedroom, kitchen, living room, and bathroom — with family members going about daily activities in each room. Flat vector illustration, warm and homey colors.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name rooms and parts of a house
- Name common furniture and household items
- Use "there is" and "there are"
- Describe your own house
- Ask "Where is the...?"

## 💬 Conversation

**Describing My House**

> **Amina:** Do you live in a big house?
> **Faisal:** No, I live in a small house. There are three rooms: a bedroom, a kitchen, and a living room.
> **Amina:** Is there a garden?
> **Faisal:** Yes, there is a small garden with flowers.
> **Amina:** That sounds nice! Where is the bathroom?
> **Faisal:** The bathroom is next to the kitchen.

[Illustration: Two students looking at a hand-drawn floor plan of Faisal's house together, pointing at different rooms, seated at a classroom desk.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation. Describe your own house.

## 📐 Grammar Point: "There is" and "There are"

We use **"there is"** for one thing, and **"there are"** for more than one:

| Singular | Plural |
|---|---|
| There is a bed in my room. | There are two beds in my room. |
| There is a garden. | There are many flowers in the garden. |

**✅ Practice:** Fill in "there is" or "there are."

1. __________ a table in the kitchen.
2. __________ three chairs in the living room.
3. __________ a mirror in the bathroom.
4. __________ two windows in my bedroom.

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Linking "there is" → "there's" in fast speech.

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat:
"There is a bed." "There's a bed."
"There are two chairs." "There are two chairs."

**Tip:** In spoken English, "there is" is often shortened to "there's," but "there are" is not shortened.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "My house has four rooms. There is a bedroom, a kitchen, a living room, and a bathroom. There is a small garden behind the house. There are two windows in my bedroom."

**Listening Questions:**
1. How many rooms are in the house?
2. Where is the garden?
3. How many windows are in the bedroom?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> This is my house. It is small but comfortable. There are three rooms: a bedroom, a kitchen, and a living room. In the living room, there is a sofa and a TV. In the kitchen, there is a stove and a fridge. I share my bedroom with my brother. I love my house.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. How many rooms are in the house?
2. What is in the living room?
3. What is in the kitchen?
4. Who does the writer share a bedroom with?
