## 💬 Conversation

**Meeting at School**

> **Teacher:** Good morning!
> **Student:** Good morning, teacher!
> **Teacher:** How are you today?
> **Student:** I am fine, thank you. And you?
> **Teacher:** I am fine too, thank you. What is your name?
> **Student:** My name is Amina.
> **Teacher:** Nice to meet you, Amina.
> **Student:** Nice to meet you too.
> **Teacher:** Welcome to SKC!
> **Student:** Thank you very much.

## 📐 Grammar Point: Greetings and Politeness Words

In English, we choose greetings based on the **time of day**:

| Time | Greeting |
|---|---|
| Morning (before 12 PM) | Good morning |
| Afternoon (12 PM–6 PM) | Good afternoon |
| Evening (after 6 PM) | Good evening |
| Bedtime | Good night |

We use **"Hello"** and **"Hi"** at any time of day.

We use polite words to be kind:
- **Please** — when we ask for something
- **Thank you** — when someone helps us
- **You're welcome** — when someone thanks us
- **Sorry** — when we make a mistake
- **Excuse me** — when we need attention or must pass by someone

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Listen to your teacher and repeat.

| Sound Focus | Words |
|---|---|
| /h/ sound | hello, hi, how |
| Long "oo" in "good" | good morning, good afternoon, good evening |
| Silent stress on "you" | thank you, see you |

**Practice Drill:**
Teacher says → Students repeat:
"Hello. Hello. Hello, how are you?"
"Good morning. Good morning. Good morning, teacher."
"Thank you. Thank you. Thank you very much."

**Tip:** In English, we often "link" words together smoothly: "Thankyou" sounds like one word, not two separate words.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud. Students listen and answer.**

*Listening Script (for teacher to read twice, at normal speed):*

> "Good morning, class! My name is Mr. Hassan. Welcome to SKC. Please sit down. Thank you."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What time of day is it? (morning / afternoon / evening)
2. What is the teacher's name?
3. What does the teacher say after "Welcome to SKC"?

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> Hello! My name is Amina. I am a new student at SKC. My teacher is Mr. Hassan. He is very kind. Every morning, I say, "Good morning, teacher." He says, "Good morning, Amina." I am happy at SKC.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What is the student's name?
2. Who is her teacher?
3. What does Amina say every morning?
4. Is Amina happy at SKC? (Yes / No)
