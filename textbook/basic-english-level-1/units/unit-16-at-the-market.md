# UNIT 16: AT THE MARKET

[Illustration: A vibrant, crowded open-air Somali market scene with vendors selling fruit, vegetables, fish, and grains from colorful stalls, camels and goats nearby, baskets piled high with produce, and shoppers bargaining cheerfully. Flat vector illustration, energetic and colorful.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name market vendors and market items
- Ask for quantities of food ("How much rice?")
- Practice bargaining politely
- Use countable and uncountable nouns
- Describe a market scene

## 💬 Conversation

**Bargaining at the Market**

> **Customer:** Good morning. How much is this rice?
> **Vendor:** This sack of rice is 400 shillings.
> **Customer:** That is expensive. Can you make it 350?
> **Vendor:** Okay, for you, 370 shillings.
> **Customer:** Alright, I will take it. Here is my money.
> **Vendor:** Thank you! Here is your rice and your change.

[Illustration: A customer and vendor bargaining over a large sack of rice at a busy market stall, both gesturing with hands, a scale visible nearby.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation using different market items and prices.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: Countable and Uncountable Nouns

**Countable nouns** can be counted (one, two, three): apples, tomatoes, eggs.
**Uncountable nouns** cannot be counted directly: rice, water, sugar, oil.

| Countable | Uncountable |
|---|---|
| How many apples? | How much rice? |
| three tomatoes | some sugar |
| five eggs | a little oil |

**✅ Practice:** Choose "how many" or "how much."

1. __________ tomatoes do you want?
2. __________ rice do you want?
3. __________ eggs are there?
4. __________ water do you need?

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Plural "-s" sounds in market words: "tomatoes," "onions," "bananas."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat:
"How many tomatoes?" "How much rice?" "Fresh fish for sale!"

**Tip:** Practice raising your voice slightly at the end of a question to sound polite when bargaining.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "At the market, I bought fresh fish, three tomatoes, and a sack of rice. The fish seller was very friendly. I paid 500 shillings for everything."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What three things did the speaker buy?
2. How many tomatoes did they buy?
3. How much did everything cost?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> Every Friday, I go to the market with my mother. We buy fresh fruit and vegetables. My mother always bargains with the vendors to get a good price. Last week, we bought five mangoes, three tomatoes, and a sack of rice. The market is crowded, but it is a fun place to visit.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. When does the writer go to the market?
2. Who does the writer go with?
3. What did they buy last week?
4. How does the writer describe the market?
