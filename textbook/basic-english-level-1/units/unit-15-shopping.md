# UNIT 15: SHOPPING

[Illustration: A busy, colorful clothing shop in a Somali town, with shelves of folded clothes, hanging dresses, a shopkeeper helping a customer try on shoes, and a cash counter with a price display. Flat vector illustration, lively commercial atmosphere.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Ask about prices ("How much is it?")
- Name clothing items
- Use numbers in shopping contexts
- Practice buying and selling dialogue
- Understand simple receipts and money vocabulary

## 💬 Conversation

**Buying Clothes**

> **Customer:** Excuse me, how much is this shirt?
> **Shopkeeper:** It is 150 shillings.
> **Customer:** That is a little expensive. Do you have a discount?
> **Shopkeeper:** For you, I can sell it for 120 shillings.
> **Customer:** Okay, I will buy it. Here is my money.
> **Shopkeeper:** Thank you! Here is your change and your receipt.
> **Customer:** Thank you very much. Goodbye!

[Illustration: A customer holding up a shirt and talking with a shopkeeper at a small clothing stall, money exchanging hands, a receipt being handed over.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation using different items and prices.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: "How much is/are...?"

We use **"How much is"** for one item, and **"How much are"** for many items:

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| How much is this shirt? | It is 100 shillings. |
| How much are these shoes? | They are 200 shillings. |

**✅ Practice:** Complete the questions and answers.

1. How much __________ (is/are) this dress? It __________ (is/are) 150 shillings.
2. How much __________ (is/are) these trousers? They __________ (is/are) 80 shillings.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Numbers combined with "shillings": "one hundred shillings," "fifty shillings."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says a price; students repeat it clearly.
"One hundred shillings." "Fifty shillings." "Two hundred shillings."

**Tip:** Say prices slowly and clearly, especially when negotiating — clear numbers avoid confusion.

---

## 🗣️ Speaking

**👥 Pair Work — Shop Role Play:** One student is the shopkeeper, one is the customer. Practice buying and selling three items with different prices.

**⭐ Speaking Challenge:** Try to negotiate a lower price politely using "Can you give me a discount?"

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "I went to the shop to buy a shirt. The shirt was 150 shillings. I also bought shoes for 300 shillings. I paid with cash and got my change."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What did the speaker buy?
2. How much was the shirt?
3. How much were the shoes?
4. How did the speaker pay?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> Yesterday, I went shopping with my sister. We went to a clothing shop near the market. I bought a blue shirt for 100 shillings. My sister bought a beautiful dress for 200 shillings. The shopkeeper was very friendly. We paid with cash and received our change and receipts.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. Who did the writer go shopping with?
2. What did the writer buy?
3. What did the sister buy?
4. How did they pay?
