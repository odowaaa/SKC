# UNIT 7: COLORS

[Illustration: A vibrant open-air market scene bursting with color — colorful fabrics (guntiino and dirac dresses) hanging on display, bright fruit stalls, and children holding balloons of many colors. Flat vector illustration, saturated and joyful color palette.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name basic colors
- Describe objects using color adjectives
- Use "It is + color" sentences
- Ask "What color is it?"
- Match colors to real-life objects

## 💬 Conversation

**Choosing Colors**

> **Shopkeeper:** Hello! Can I help you?
> **Customer:** Yes, please. I want a scarf.
> **Shopkeeper:** What color do you like?
> **Customer:** I like blue. Do you have a blue scarf?
> **Shopkeeper:** Yes! Here is a light blue scarf and a dark blue scarf.
> **Customer:** I like the dark blue one. How much is it?
> **Shopkeeper:** It is 100 shillings.
> **Customer:** Okay, I will take it. Thank you.

[Illustration: A colorful fabric shop with many scarves hanging in different colors, a shopkeeper showing two blue scarves to a customer, warm market atmosphere.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation using different colors and items.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: "It is" + Color / Adjectives

We use **"It is"** to describe one object:

- It is **red**.
- It is **a red apple**.
- The apple **is red**.

We can also describe more than one object using **"They are"**:

- They are **green**.
- The leaves **are green**.

**✅ Practice:** Complete the sentences.

1. The sky __________ blue. (is/are)
2. The leaves __________ green. (is/are)
3. My bag __________ black. (is/are)

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Vowel sounds in color words: "blue" /uː/, "green" /iː/, "yellow" /ɛ/.

**Practice Drill:** Teacher shows a color card; students say the color name aloud quickly.

"Red, red, red." "Blue, blue, blue." "Green, green, green."

**Tip:** Practice the difference between "green" and "grey" — they sound similar but are different colors.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "Look at the flag. It has blue and white. The Somali flag is blue with a white star in the middle."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What two colors are mentioned?
2. What shape is white on the flag?
3. What is the main color of the flag?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> I like colors. My favorite color is green. My shirt is green, and my bag is also green. My friend Amina likes pink. Her scarf is pink, and her shoes are pink too. We both like bright colors.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What is the writer's favorite color?
2. What color is the writer's bag?
3. What is Amina's favorite color?
4. What color are Amina's shoes?

[Illustration: Two friends, one dressed head-to-toe in green (shirt and bag), the other in pink (scarf and shoes), standing together smiling, matching the reading passage.]

## 🎲 Games

**Game 1 — Color Race:** Teacher says a color. Students race to touch something that color in the classroom.

**Game 2 — Color Bingo:** Students color a 9-square bingo card with different colors. Teacher calls colors aloud; students mark matching squares.
