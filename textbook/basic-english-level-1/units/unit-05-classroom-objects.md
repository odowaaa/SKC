# UNIT 5: CLASSROOM OBJECTS

[Illustration: A bright, organized SKC classroom filled with labeled objects — desks, chairs, a whiteboard, a bookshelf, a clock on the wall, and a bag hanging on a chair. An adult student is arranging their desk with a notebook and pen. Flat vector illustration, teal and gold color palette.]

### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Name common classroom objects
- Ask "What is this?" and "Where is the...?"
- Use "this," "that," "these," "those"
- Follow simple classroom instructions
- Locate objects using simple prepositions

## 💬 Conversation

**In the Classroom**

> **Teacher:** What is this?
> **Student:** This is a book.
> **Teacher:** Good. What is that, near the window?
> **Student:** That is a clock.
> **Teacher:** Excellent. Where is your pen?
> **Student:** My pen is in my bag.
> **Teacher:** Please take out your notebook and pen.
> **Student:** Okay, teacher.

[Illustration: A teacher holding up a book, asking a student a question, while the student's desk shows a bag, notebook, and pen. Warm, well-lit classroom scene.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation using different classroom objects.

---

## 📐 Grammar Point: This / That / These / Those

We use these words to point to things:

| Word | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| this | one object, near | This is a pen. |
| that | one object, far | That is a board. |
| these | many objects, near | These are books. |
| those | many objects, far | Those are chairs. |

**✅ Practice:** Choose "this," "that," "these," or "those."

1. __________ is my bag. (near, one)
2. __________ are your pencils. (near, many)
3. __________ is the door. (far, one)
4. __________ are the windows. (far, many)

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** The "th" sound in "this," "that," "these," "those."

**Practice Drill:** Put your tongue between your teeth and say: "th... th... this... that... these... those."

Teacher says → Students repeat:
"This is a pen." "That is a board." "These are books." "Those are chairs."

**Tip:** The "th" sound does not exist in Somali. Practice slowly in front of a mirror.

## 🗣️ Speaking

**👥 Pair Work — Object Hunt:** Point to five classroom objects and say a full sentence for each: "This is a ______."

**⭐ Speaking Challenge:** Describe your desk. What is on it? What is under it?

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "Look at the classroom. There is a board, a clock, and a map on the wall. There are twenty desks and twenty chairs. There is a bookshelf near the door."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What is on the wall?
2. How many desks are there?
3. What is near the door?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> This is my classroom at SKC. It is clean and bright. There is a big whiteboard at the front. There are twenty desks and chairs. My bag, my book, and my pen are on my desk. I like my classroom.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. Where is the whiteboard?
2. How many desks and chairs are there?
3. What three things are on the student's desk?

[Illustration: A tidy, cheerful classroom illustration matching the reading passage — whiteboard at front, rows of desks and chairs, and one desk in the foreground with a bag, book, and pen clearly visible.]

## ✍️ Writing

**1. Trace and copy:**

`b o o k` ______ `p e n` ______ `d e s k` ______ `c h a i r` ______

**2. Label the picture:** Write the correct word under each object.

[Illustration: A worksheet showing six unlabeled classroom objects (book, pen, bag, chair, clock, window) with blank lines beneath each for students to write the word.]

**3. Fill in the blanks:**

- This is my __________. (point to your book)
- __________ are my pencils.
- The clock is on the __________.

**4. Sentence Writing:** Write two sentences about your own desk using "this" and "these."
