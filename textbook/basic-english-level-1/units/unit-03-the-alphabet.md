### 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:
- Recognize and say all 26 letters of the English alphabet
- Identify capital (uppercase) and small (lowercase) letters
- Identify vowels and consonants
- Spell simple words aloud
- Write the alphabet in order

## 💬 Conversation

**Learning to Spell**

> **Teacher:** Let's practice the alphabet. Can you say it with me?
> **Student:** Yes! A, B, C, D, E, F, G...
> **Teacher:** Very good! Now, can you spell your name?
> **Student:** Yes. My name is Ali. A-L-I.
> **Teacher:** Excellent! Is "A" a vowel or a consonant?
> **Student:** "A" is a vowel.
> **Teacher:** Correct! Well done.

[Illustration: A teacher standing beside a whiteboard with the alphabet written on it, pointing to letters while an adult student, Ali, practices spelling his name aloud with a raised hand. Bright, encouraging classroom scene.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation. Spell your own name for your partner.

## 📐 Grammar Point: Using "A" and "An"

We use **"a"** before a consonant sound, and **"an"** before a vowel sound.

| A + Consonant | An + Vowel |
|---|---|
| a book | an apple |
| a pen | an egg |
| a cat | an orange |
| a dog | an umbrella |

**✅ Practice:** Write "a" or "an" before each word.

1. ______ apple
2. ______ book
3. ______ egg
4. ______ pen
5. ______ orange
6. ______ dog

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** Letter names vs. letter sounds.

| Letter | Letter Name | Letter Sound |
|---|---|---|
| B | "bee" | /b/ as in "book" |
| C | "see" | /k/ as in "cat" |
| S | "es" | /s/ as in "sun" |

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says a letter name; students say a word that starts with that letter.
"A" → "Apple." "B" → "Ball." "C" → "Cat."

**Tip:** Some letters sound similar. Practice the difference between "B" (bee) and "V" (vee), and between "M" (em) and "N" (en).

## 🗣️ Speaking

**👥 Pair Work — Letter Quiz:** One student says a letter. The partner says a word that begins with that letter. Switch roles.

**⭐ Speaking Challenge:** Say the entire alphabet from A to Z without stopping, in under 20 seconds.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud slowly.**

*Listening Script:*

> "Listen and write the letters: A... M... I... N... A. What word do these letters spell?"

**Listening Questions:**
1. Write the letters you hear in order.
2. What name do the letters spell?
3. Is the first letter a vowel or a consonant?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> The English alphabet has 26 letters. Five letters are vowels: A, E, I, O, U. The other 21 letters are consonants. We use letters to make words. We use words to make sentences.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. How many letters are in the alphabet?
2. How many vowels are there?
3. Name two consonants.

## 🎲 Games

**Game 1 — Alphabet Race:** In teams, students race to write the alphabet correctly on the board, one letter per student.

**Game 2 — Letter Bingo:** Give each student a card with 9 random letters. Teacher calls out letters. First student to complete a row says "Bingo!"
