## 📐 Grammar Point: Simple Present Tense for Routines

We use the **simple present tense** to talk about things we do regularly:

| Subject | Verb Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I / You / We / They | base verb | I study English every day. |
| He / She / It | verb + s | She studies English every day. |

**✅ Practice:** Complete the sentences with the correct verb form.

1. I __________ (go) to school every day.
2. She __________ (study) math on Tuesdays.
3. The teacher __________ (teach) English.
4. We __________ (eat) lunch at noon.

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** The "-s" ending sound in third person singular verbs: "studies," "teaches," "goes."

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says → Students repeat:
"She studies English." "He teaches math." "The bell rings at eight."

**Tip:** The "-s" ending can sound like /s/, /z/, or /ɪz/ depending on the final sound of the verb.

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud twice.**

*Listening Script:*

> "My school is SKC. My classroom is next to the library. My favorite subject is English. The bell rings at eight o'clock, and class starts right away. I eat lunch in the cafeteria at noon."

**Listening Questions:**
1. What is the name of the school?
2. Where is the classroom located?
3. What time does the bell ring?
4. Where does the speaker eat lunch?

---

## 📚 Reading

**Read the short text.**

> I study at SKC. My school has a classroom, a library, an office, and a cafeteria. My favorite subject is English because I want to speak with many people. My teacher is very patient. Every day, I do my homework before class. I am proud to be an SKC student.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What places does the school have?
2. What is the writer's favorite subject? Why?
3. What does the writer do before class?
4. How does the writer feel about being an SKC student?
