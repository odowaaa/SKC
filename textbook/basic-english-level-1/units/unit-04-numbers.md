## 💬 Conversation

**At the Shop**

> **Shopkeeper:** Hello! How can I help you?
> **Customer:** I want five oranges, please.
> **Shopkeeper:** Okay. That is five oranges. The price is ten shillings.
> **Customer:** Here is ten shillings.
> **Shopkeeper:** Thank you! Here are your five oranges.
> **Customer:** Thank you very much. Goodbye.

[Illustration: A small Somali fruit shop with a shopkeeper handing a paper bag of oranges to a customer, a chalkboard sign showing prices with numbers, warm marketplace colors.]

**👥 Pair Work:** Practice this conversation. Change the number of items and the price.

## 📐 Grammar Point: "How many" + Plural Nouns

We use **"How many"** to ask about quantity. The noun after "how many" is usually **plural** (add -s).

- How many **books** do you have? — I have three **books**.
- How many **pens** are there? — There are five **pens**.
- How many **students** are in class? — There are twenty **students**.

**✅ Practice:** Complete the questions and answers.

1. How many chairs? — There are ______ chairs. (4)
2. How many apples? — There are ______ apples. (7)
3. How many students? — There are ______ students. (15)

## 🔊 Pronunciation

**Sound Focus:** The "-teen" vs "-ty" ending.

| -teen (13–19) | -ty (30–90) |
|---|---|
| thirTEEN (stress at end) | THIRty (stress at start) |
| fourTEEN | FORty |
| sixTEEN | SIXty |

**Practice Drill:** Teacher says a pair; students say which one they hear.
"Thirteen or thirty?" "Fourteen or forty?" "Sixteen or sixty?"

**Tip:** Listen carefully — these number pairs sound similar but mean very different amounts!

## 👂 Listening

**Teacher reads aloud.**

*Listening Script:*

> "My phone number is zero-six-one, two-two-three, four-four-five-five. I have three brothers and two sisters. I am twenty-five years old."

**Listening Questions:**
1. Write the phone number you hear.
2. How many brothers does the speaker have?
3. How many sisters?
4. How old is the speaker?

## 🎲 Games

**Game 1 — Number Bingo:** Each student gets a card with 9 numbers (1–50). Teacher calls numbers aloud. First to complete a row calls "Bingo!"

**Game 2 — Counting Circle:** Students stand in a circle and count aloud one by one. Anyone who makes a mistake starts the count over.
